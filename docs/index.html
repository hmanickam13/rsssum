Summary: Chinese gold demand improved in August, with gold withdrawals from the Shanghai Gold Exchange (SGE) totaling 161 tons, a 46-ton increase from the previous month. Despite a decline in the price of gold in dollar terms, it was up 1.8% in yuan due to Chinese currency weakness. Gold has outperformed most other assets in China, leading to strong wholesale and retail demand. Chinese investors are turning to gold as a safe-haven asset, with metal flowing into Chinese gold-backed ETFs for the third consecutive month. The People's Bank of China also added 29 tons of gold to its reserves in August, marking the 10th straight month of gold buying. However, high gold prices could pose challenges for the Chinese gold market. The World Gold Council predicts that an improved outlook for China's economy and upcoming jewelry fairs and industry events may support local gold demand.

You Should Be Very Worried About the Digital Services Act

Link: https://quoththeraven.substack.com/p/you-should-be-very-worried-about

Summary: The recently enacted Digital Services Act (DSA) in the European Union (EU) poses a significant threat to freedom of speech. Under the DSA, the European Commission has the power to restrict citizens' ability to engage in democratic discourse on digital platforms. The Act empowers the Commission to pressure platforms to curb "hate speech," "disinformation," and threats to "civic discourse," which are vague and subjective categories. The legislation gives the Commission discretionary powers to oversee content moderation policies, effectively holding freedom of speech hostage to the ideological preferences of unelected officials. The Act also creates "trusted flaggers" who can report illegal content, and platforms are required to prioritize their reports. Non-compliance with the Act can result in hefty fines, and periodic audits by the Commission can pressure platforms to police speech in a way that aligns with the Commission's preferred narrative. The Act grants the Commission broad power and discretion, allowing it to declare emergencies and demand interventions by platforms. The DSA lacks clear definitions and checks and balances, making it a potential weapon for the Commission's political agenda. Overall, the Act undermines freedom of expression and places too much power in the hands of unelected officials.
